Why I Got Into Sourdough Baking

I started baking sourdough at home about two years ago and it's become a weekly thing. There's something satisfying about turning three ingredients into bread that rivals what you'd buy at a bakery.

What you actually need

The good news: the equipment list is short. You can start with what's already in your kitchen.

  • A kitchen scale (don't use cup measurements for bread)
  • A large mixing bowl
  • A Dutch oven or covered cast iron pot
  • An active sourdough starter

Where to learn more

The best free resource I've found is The Perfect Loaf. It explains the science as well as the steps.

Why bother?

Honestly? It's slow. A single loaf takes about 24 hours from starting the dough to slicing it. But most of that is waiting, not working. Active time is maybe 20 minutes spread across a day. And the result is better than most supermarket bread.
